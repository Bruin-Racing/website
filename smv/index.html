AN OVERVIEW

Bruin SuperMileage builds and competes with two vehicles every year: an Electric Vehicle (EV) and an Internal Combustion or Gas Vehicle (GV). We compete in the collegiate Shell Eco-marathon Americas competition, where we are challenged to design, build, and race the most fuel-efficient vehicle as possible. We strive to push the boundaries of what is technically possible, giving students invaluable experience and real world engineering skills. With a focus on improving energy efficiency, we help reduce the harmful environmental effects of gasoline combustion.

The work we do can be split up into 4 co-dependent categories: mechanical, electrical, software, and composites. Within the two vehicle teams, that work is split between Powertrain, Electrical, Steering, Structures, and EV Special Projects.
